Welcome Note

WHO is a specialized agency of the United Nations (UN), which was established in the year 1946 with the objective of attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health. For achieving the objective, the organization, among others, performs functions like directing and coordinating in international health works, maintaining effective collaboration with UN and its specialized agencies, Government health administration, professional groups and such other organizations, assisting countries in strengthening health services through appropriate technical assistance and aid upon request of the Governments, and stimulating and advancing work for prevention control and eradication of epidemic, endemic and other diseases. All members of the United Nations (UN) are regarded as members of the organization. The work of the organization is carried out by its three organs, namely; The World Health Assembly (WHA), The Executive Board and The Secretariat..

The WHA is composed of delegates representing Member States. The Assembly meets in regular annual session and in such special session as may be necessary. During sessions, the WHA takes resolutions and decisions for accomplishing its assigned functions. The Executive Board (EB), consisting of 34 Members designated by as many Member States, acts as the executive organ of the WHA. The main function of EB is to give effects to the decisions and policies of the Assembly as well as to submit advice or proposal to the Assembly on its own initiative.

The Secretariat of the organization comprises the Director General (DG) and such other technical administrative staff as the organization may require. The DG is appointed by the WHA on the nomination of EB. The DG is the ex officio secretary of the WHA, EB and all other commissions and committees, unless such function is delegated by the DG.

For effectively managing the regional matters of the organization, six regional organizations have been established by the WHA to meet special needs of each region. Each regional organization consists of a Regional Committee (RC) and a Regional Office. RC is composed of representatives of the Member States of that region. Bangladesh is placed in the South East Asia Region (SEAR). RC usually meets once in a year. The main assigned function of RC is to formulate policies and actions on health issues of regional character. Head of the Regional office is the Regional Director (RD) appointed by the EB in agreement of the recommendation from RC. Regional Office is the administrative organ of the regional committee and also carries out resolutions and decisions of the WHA and the EB within the region.

Resolutions taken in sessions of WHA, EB and RC of WHO are important guiding document for each of the Member State. Bangladesh, being an active member in all sessions of high-level meetings of WHO, recognizes national, regional and international implications of those resolutions, recommendations and decisions for health improvement. Hence, a database with resolutions adopted in high-level meetings of the organization from 2009-2013 has been developed for use by interested persons, institutions and organizations.
